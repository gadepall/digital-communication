Question 12.13.3.27:A biased die is such that $\pr{4} = \frac{1}{10}$ and other scores being equally likely. The die is tossed twice. If X is the 'number of fours seen', find the variance of the random variable X.\\
%\fi
\solution
Since, X = number of fours seen on tossing a die twice, X=\cbrak{0,1,2}\\
Also,
\begin{align}
\pr{4} = \frac{1}{10}\\
\pr{4^\prime} = \frac{9}{10}
\end{align}
So,
\begin{align}
p_X(k)&= \comb{2}{k} \pr{4}^k \pr{4^\prime}^{2-k}\\
&=
\begin{cases}
\comb{2}{0}\pr{4^\prime}^2=\frac{81}{100} & k=0\\
\comb{2}{1}\pr{4}\pr{4^\prime}=\frac{18}{100} & k=1\\
\comb{2}{2}\pr{4}^2=\frac{1}{100} & k=2
\end{cases}
\end{align}
\begin{table}[H]
        \centering
        \input{tables/main.tex}
        \caption{Random Variables}
        \label{tab:12.13.3.27}
\end{table}
\begin{align}
Var(X) &= E(X^2)-\brak{E(X)}^2\\
Var(X)&= \sum k^2p_X(k) - \brak{\sum kp_X(k)}^2\\
&=\brak{0+\frac{18}{100}+\frac{4}{100}}-\brak{0+\frac{18}{100}+\frac{2}{100}}^2\\
&=\frac{22}{100}-\brak{\frac{20}{100}}^2\\
&=\frac{11}{50}-\frac{1}{25}\\
&=\frac{9}{50}\\
Var(X)&=0.18
\end{align}
