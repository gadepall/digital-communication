Question 12.13.3.63\\
A and B are events such that $\pr{A} = 0.4$ and $\pr{B} = 0.3$ and $\pr{A+B} = 0.5$ .Then $\pr{B^\prime A}$ is equal to\\
\solution
\fi
By axioms,
\begin{align}
	A &= A (B + B^\prime)\\
	A &= AB + AB^\prime \\
	\implies \pr{A} &= \pr{AB} + \pr{AB^\prime} \\
	\implies \pr{AB^\prime} &= \pr{A} - \pr{AB}
\end{align}
we also know that,
\begin{align}
	\pr{AB} &= \pr{A} + \pr{B} -\pr{A+B}\\
	&= 0.4 + 0.3 - 0.5\\
	&= 0.2
\end{align}
Hence,
\begin{align}
	\pr{AB^\prime} &= 0.4 - 0.2\\
	&= 0.2
\end{align}
